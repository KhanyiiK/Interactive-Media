Reflection on the reading and understanding of UI/UX
A work on the subject of context's importance in literary interpretation is Standard and Context,
by Mailloux, published in 1983. It investigates how reading a work in a particular setting can have
an impact on conventions, or the typical methods of understanding a text. According to Mailloux,
context is essential to understanding a document. Conventions are not fixed; rather, they can be
interpreted in different ways depending on the reader's situation. Additionally, he talks about the
connections between social and literary norms, emphasizing the value of knowing a text's social setting
in order to fully comprehend its meaning. As learning Web developers we can learn a range of principles
about designing user interfaces that work from Mailloux (1983). Mailloux concentrates on the use of
conventions in user interfaces to establish a feeling of context that can aid users in comprehending the
goal and functionality of a website or application. He places emphasis on how conventions can be used
to improve the usability of a website or application and can help users make choices and direct their behavior.
