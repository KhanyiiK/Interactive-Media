My experience continuing the development of my website:.
In all honesty, despite the frustrations I've experienced when my
design or code refused to cooperate with me, I discovered that I genuinely
liked developing my website. I genuinely built a website from scratch, which
is truly mind-boggling because I never imagined I'd be able to do so. I had to
update certain components of my website, but the adjustments were minor because they
only comprised modest navigation choices that I wanted to add. I needed to do this
since I needed to consider UX and UI. Some layouts seemed better in theory but not in practice.

IxD Process:
All the information required for this question can be found in the Design Section
I considered the flow of my website. As a way of simple navigation for the user,
I aim to have a header that is continuously visible (at least in desktop mode).
I'm aiming to make each of my pages internally familiar, since this will help the
user rapidly become acclimated to the structure of my website, and I'll employ
exterior familiarity features to provide the user with a smooth experience when
browsing my website for the first time.

Reflection on this weeks readings?
After reading Hassenzahl and Tractinsky's 2006 work "User Experience - A Research Agenda,"
user experience design is the major issue. The reading gave an informative review of user
experience in regard to traditional usability problems in order to investigate the emotional
and social components of user engagement with products and services. The author's definition of
UX as a "momentary, primarily feeling" of engaging with a product or service helped me understand
that UX is about more than simply functionality, but also the user's emotional response to the encounter.

References:
Hassenzahl, M. & Tractinsky, N., 2006. User experience-a research agenda. Behaviour & information
technology, 25(2), pp.91-97.
